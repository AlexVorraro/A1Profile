Overview

The project idea that I am presenting is the development of a videogame. It is a co-op campaign with 5 people playing online together. This could be developed as a first person game or as a third person game. The game’s plot should be semirealistic, so without magical or fantastic event but with unlucky and improbable things happening. It is considerable a strategical action game. Its main target is to put all the team together with some fun gameplay that includes collaborating and coordinating attack and defence to obtain the victory of the game.

Motivation

Strategy games have been very popular during the years. An example is Tom Clancy’s Rainbow Six Siege that sold over 10 million copies worldwide. While the fun of playing in company of your friends in priceless. These two characteristics, added to a good and strong plot is what makes my game enjoyable and interesting. The absence of ranks instead makes this the perfect game to play when you need a break from your sweaty, try-hard game and you just want to have some fun with your friends.

Description

The main features of this game are: a strong original plot, that motivates the players to see how it ends, social features, that enable group collaboration and coordination, a fun gameplay that includes a combat system and other minigames and great music that make scenes memorable The game starts with the selection of the available characters. Once everyone has chosen their character the story starts. The main characters meet for a few moments one night in one of the bars in the city. They all have huge amounts of debts with the same company, but nobody is aware of the other’s problems. When they return home, they all receive the same phone call from the company that lent them money telling them that there is the opportunity to cancel all their debts and actually earn some money by living and working for three months in a mine with 29 other people whilst being recorded for a tv show. Nobody really understood what that meant but they were sure that it was their opportunity to escape this dead end. When they arrive at the mine they are all amazed at seeing each other. The owner of the mine arrives and explains that their daily tasks will take more or less 16 hours and that every day of work they will receive a ticket, and at 90 tickets they will be free. They decide to work together, reducing the stress and making thing easier. After the second day the owner of the mine lets everyone notice that in the rules of the tv show it says that only half of the participants will have their debts cancelled and that if they leave now their debts will rise more than if they lose the game. After that he goes away without giving any explanation. In that moment they can’t understand how the winners were going to be chosen, but when one of the members had all of his tickets stolen the next day, they understand that war had begun. They begin to collaborate as a group to defend themselves from the other 25 members. People start betting their tickets, and this brings the user to play a few different minigames that could help win or make him lose even more tickets. The user is mainly involved in the 8 night hours that become the action time. They are supposed to defend what they have and win as many tickets as possible by organizing strategy assaults against the other groups that have formed and, including fighting and stealth moments, and defending strategies. There is also a stamina bar that changes your speed and strength. You can restore your stamina in the training room, where you can also unlock new combat techniques if in company with someone by doing timing skill checks. This means that it is important to organize your training time as well as the rest of the activities. The game ends with a victory if all 5 can pass through the 15 winning participants. If even only one can’t pass through, the team loses.

Tools and Technologies

To develop my video game, I don’t need a big team or a huge budget like many would think. I need a game engine, like ‘Unreal’, that is intended for 3D games. This game engine if for free but there is a small percentage of royalty owned if the game’s gross if over $3000. I would then produce my own 3D models on ‘Blender’, that is also free. The minimum hardware requirements for the two software are 8gb of Ram, a Quad-core CPU and for a GPU at least an NVIDIA GeForce 470 GTX or AMD Radeon 6870 HD series card.

Skills Required

There are many free video courses offered by ‘Epic Games’, the developer itself, that show you how to move within the software. This is also true for 'Blender' that is full of tutorials on Youtube that teach step by step what to do. On Unreal you can use C++ language or you can use the programming language of Unreal, ‘Blueprints’. Learning C++ shouldn’t be as difficult for me because I already know how to program in C, while learning how to create a 3D model is totally different. It takes a lot of time and experience to learn all the different necessary steps that are behind the 3D designing.

Outcome

If the project is successful my videogame will be created and published on a store. That would mean that I would have learnt how to program and how to produce a 3D model by studying and following tutorials and well-done articles that are on the web. The next step would be to follow all the feedback that the players have sent back, check that there are no bugs in the game and if there are, resolve them. This program development and design experience would give me the skills that could be re-utilised and expanded upon in any future job.
